CHURN PREDICTION MODEL¶

INTRODUCTION¶

Churn Prediction refers to the process of identifying customers who are likely to stop using a company’s products or services. In business, particularly in subscription-based services or retail, customer churn is a critical metric as it directly impacts revenue. The goal of a churn prediction model is to accurately forecast which customers are at risk of leaving, allowing the company to take proactive measures to retain them.

Why Feature Selection with RFE?

To reduce the complexity of the model. To improve the model's accuracy by selecting only the most important features. To enhance the model's interpretability.

The Churn Prediction Model project aimed to identify customers at risk of discontinuing their relationship with the bank. Through a systematic approach encompassing data exploration, preprocessing, modeling, and evaluation, several key insights and outcomes were achieved:

1. Data Exploration

  • Dataset Overview: The dataset contains various features related to customer demographics and transactions, including attributes like age, income, credit limit, and churn status.
  • Data Quality: The dataset was clean with no missing values, facilitating a smooth preprocessing phase.

2. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

  • Customer Demographics: The majority of customers are between 30 and 60 years old. Most customers fall into the 'High' income category.
  • Churn Analysis: The churn rate was approximately 15%, indicating a moderate level of customer attrition.

3. Preprocessing and Feature Engineering

  • Encoding: Categorical variables were transformed into numerical format using one-hot encoding.
  • Feature Engineering: No additional feature engineering was applied beyond encoding, as the existing features were deemed sufficient.

4. Model Training and Evaluation

  • Model Used: A Random Forest classifier was chosen for its robustness and ability to handle complex datasets.
  • Performance Metrics:
    1. Accuracy: The model achieved an accuracy of X% on the test set.
    2. Precision: Precision for detecting churn was Y%, reflecting the accuracy of positive churn predictions.
    3. Recall: Recall for detecting churn was Z%, showing the model’s ability to identify actual churn cases.
    4. F1 Score: The F1 score was W%, balancing precision and recall.
    5. Confusion Matrix: Provided insight into the model’s performance with true positives, false positives, true negatives, and false negatives.
